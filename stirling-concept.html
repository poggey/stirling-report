The daily briefing · desk note

Hot American inflation resets the summer; British pay data pulls the other way

Thursday 2 July 2026 · Edition Nº 214 · Overcast
UK 10Y ▲11bp → 4.42%GBP/USD ▼0.62%Brent ▼1.8%Gold ▲0.7%

Yesterday's US core CPI print of 0.4% on the month — a tenth above consensus — was enough to unwind most of June's dovish repricing. Treasuries led: the US 10-year added 9 basis points to 4.61%, its highest since mid-May, and gilts followed at the London open, the UK 10-year rising 11 basis points to 4.42%.

The cross-current arrived at seven o'clock. Wage growth slowed to 4.4% against 4.7% expected, with unemployment ticking up to 4.6%. The front end of the gilt curve rallied on the print even as the long end stayed heavy — a textbook divergence between imported term-premium pressure and softening domestic conditions. Markets now price the next Bank Rate cut a meeting earlier than they did yesterday.

Sterling sat squarely in the crossfire, breaking below 1.26 as rate differentials moved against it from both directions. Equities were calm on the surface; beneath it, defensives outperformed, and the FTSE's gain owed more to the weaker pound than to genuine risk appetite.

  2. OPEC+ meets Sunday; watch Brent positioning into the weekend
  3. The Bank's Chief Economist speaks at 10:15 — first reaction to today's wage print
